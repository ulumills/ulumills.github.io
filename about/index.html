Currently I’m living in Pittsburgh and pursuing my master’s degree in Design for Interactions at Carnegie Mellon University. I was born in Philadelphia, brought up on Maui, had a theater career in Alaska and a teaching career in Japan. I love languages, storytelling, and the awkward navigation of cultural intersections, which have had a strong influence on how I work as a designer.

I’m interested in technology’s impact on senses of cultural identity and community. I believe that design can be leveraged as a powerful tool for easing these cultural shifts—design that is not just human-centered, but humanity-centered. Taking a holistic attitude in the design of everyday products and services, however innocuous and at whatever level of scale, will lead to a more conscientious, more connected, more empathetic world.

I spend my free time playing Japanese folk drums with Pittsburgh Taiko, riding my bike, playing board games, and choosing perfect avocados (my unmarketable superpower). And posing for the occasional selfie with an obliging marsupial.
